Arrive 4:00

⛪ Manaoag Church
📍 Milo St, Manaoag, Pangasinan
📍 View Map

The Minor Basilica of Our Lady of the Rosary of Manaoag is one of the most venerated Marian shrines in the Philippines — and a beloved stop for road-tripping devotees. At 4 AM you'll find the church already open and other pilgrims quietly in prayer, creating an incredibly peaceful and moving atmosphere. Rest, stretch your legs, have your baon breakfast in the grounds, and offer a prayer for safe travels. ⭐ 4.8/5 from 10,000+ visitors.

🆓 Free entrance 🕓 Opens 4:00 AM daily ⭐ 4.8 · 10,202 reviews
💡 Tips & Notes
  • Dress modestly — shoulders and knees covered inside the basilica
  • Candles available for purchase near the church entrance (₱10–50)
  • No pet-friendly — leave furry friends in the car with windows cracked
  • Parking is available in pay lots around the church (₱30–50/hr)
  • This is your breakfast stop — eat your baon at the grounds or nearby benches
  • Souvenir shops open early for rosaries, statues, and pasalubong

🥩 Steak ni Juan (Steak to Juan ELYU)
📍 Gen. Peralta St, Luna, La Union (inside Baluarte area)
📍 View Map

Your first proper meal in La Union! Steak to Juan (also known as Steak ni Juan ELYU) is tucked just behind the Baluarte watchtower area in Luna. It's known for incredibly affordable, quality steak dishes that punch way above their price. The rustic, laid-back vibe overlooking the scenery makes brunch here especially satisfying after the long drive. Expect friendly stray dogs around — they're well-cared for by the owner. ⭐ 4.2/5 rating.

💰 Budget: ₱200–400/person ⭐ 4.2 rating 🎟️ Environmental fee may apply at Baluarte
🥩 Rib Eye – ₱270 🍳 Sizzling Bagnet Sisig – ₱200 🍔 STJ Classic Burger – ₱170 🧋 Brewed Iced Tea (Pitcher) – ₱90 🌭 Hungarian Sausage (must-try!)
💡 Tips

  • Baluarte is a Spanish-era fortification overlooking the La Union coast
  • Best to arrive early — seating can fill up on weekends
  • Cash preferred; card acceptance may be limited

1:00 PM Bahay
na Bato
🪨 Bahay na Bato + Pebble Beach
📍 Luna, La Union
📍 View Map

One of La Union's most unique cultural gems — Bahay na Bato is a charming compound built using pebbles and stones sourced from the beach. Inside you'll find art galleries, sculptures, antique décor, and a rustic café where you can sip drinks with the sound of the ocean just steps away. The adjacent Pebble Beach is incredibly therapeutic — the sound of smooth stones underfoot and the ocean breeze make this a perfect post-brunch chill spot. ⭐ 4.6/5.

🕓 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM ⭐ 4.6 · 307 reviews 🆓 Free to enter (café purchases encouraged)
💡 Tips
  • Wear comfortable sandals — the pebble beach is uneven underfoot
  • Great spot to buy handcrafted souvenirs and local art
  • The café has refreshments perfect for the afternoon heat
  • Golden hour light here is stunning for photos
  • Phone: +63 912 672 6099 — call ahead if you want to confirm opening

🌲 Almeida Man-Made Forest
📍 Bacnotan-Luna-Balaoan Rd, Balaoan, La Union
📍 View Map

A magical canopy of towering mahogany trees lining both sides of the highway — the Almeida Man-Made Forest is a quick but memorable stop. The cool shade, green tunnel effect, and relative quiet make it a refreshing detour and a great photo opportunity. Best for a 15–20 minute breather, stretching, and some snaps. ⭐ 4.6/5.

🆓 Free — roadside stop 🕓 Open 24 hours ⭐ 4.6 · 273 reviews
💡 Safety Tips

  • Park on the side of the road safely; hazard lights on
  • Best for a short 15-minute stopover — quick, cool, and green
  • Locals sell snacks along the roadside

🏰 The Mansion Estates La Union
📍 Upper Talogtog, San Juan, La Union
📍 View Map

Perched high on the hills of San Juan, The Mansion Estates offers one of the most breathtaking overlooking views of the La Union coastline and San Juan Beach. The coffee shop is open (with limited food options), and the view — especially as the afternoon sun starts its descent — is absolutely worth the steep climb. A perfect spot to enjoy 2+ hours before the beach sunset. ⭐ 4.5/5.

☕ Coffee: ~₱100–200 ⭐ 4.5 · 66 reviews ⚠️ Very steep road — careful driving required
💡 Tips
  • The road up is very steep with 3 extremely sharp inclines — make sure your vehicle is in good condition
  • There are separate pedestrian stairs if you prefer to walk up
  • Go before sunset — the golden hour view over San Juan Beach is iconic
  • Coffee shop is open; hotel/restaurant reportedly opening soon in 2026 — check ahead
  • Fresh mountain air + coastal view = perfect afternoon refresh before beach time

🌊 Urbiztondo Beach + Kabsat Dinner
📍 Urbiztondo, San Juan, La Union
📍 View Map

Welcome to ELYU — the crown jewel of your trip! Urbiztondo Beach in San Juan is the surfing capital of Northern Luzon, lined with lively beach bars, surf shacks, and incredible food spots. Park at the Elyu Parking Area (~₱50–100) and spend the evening exploring. Kabsat is a local favorite for dinner right by the waves — expect fresh seafood, cold drinks, and that unmistakable beach-night atmosphere. ⭐ Urbiztondo: 4.4/5 from 382 reviews.

🅿️ Parking: ~₱50–100 🍽️ Kabsat Dinner: ~₱200–400/person 🕓 Beach open 24 hours ⭐ 4.4 · 382 reviews
💡 Tips for the Evening
  • Walk the strip after dinner — lots of bars and chill spots to explore
  • Kabsat is known for its local vibe and affordable beach food
  • Other recommended spots: NakNak, El Union Coffee, Flotsam & Jetsam
  • Keep an eye on belongings in crowded areas
  • The beach is great for a night swim — but go with company and be cautious of waves
  • Don't miss this as a preview before tomorrow morning's surf lesson!

🚗 ~20 min drive to Urbiztondo, San Juan
7:00 AM Surf
🏄
🏄 Surfing at William's Surf School
📍 Urbiztondo, San Juan, La Union
📍 View Map

The highlight of the entire trip — surf lessons at one of Urbiztondo's well-regarded surf schools! William's Surf School (and the broader Urbiztondo surf schools like La Union Surf School nearby) are great for beginners and intermediates alike. Morning sessions (7–9 AM) are ideal: smaller crowds, calmer winds, and the magic of catching waves as the sun rises. Expect friendly, patient instructors.

💰 Surf Lessons: ~₱500–800/person (1–2 hrs) 🏄 Board rental: ~₱150–200/hr (after lesson) 🕓 Schools open from 7:00 AM
💡 Surf Tips
  • Book in advance or arrive early — instructors fill up quickly on weekends
  • Morning waves at Urbiztondo are best for beginners — typically 1–2 ft
  • Wear rash guard to avoid surf rash and sun damage
  • Don't be discouraged if you wipe out — everyone does on day one!
  • After the lesson, free swim and bodyboarding are fair game
  • Keep valuables locked in your car or at a trusted beach shack

🛕 Ma-Cho Temple (Taoist Temple)
📍 Quezon Ave, Pagdaraoan, San Fernando City, La Union
📍 View Map

An absolute gem of San Fernando — the Ma-Cho Temple is a beautifully maintained Taoist temple perched above the city with stunning views of the South China Sea. Free incense sticks are provided for prayers, and the wide courtyard and high-ceilinged temple interior are stunning. One of the most photographed spots in the region. ⭐ 4.5/5 from 1,642 reviews.

🆓 Free entrance (donations welcome) 🕓 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM ⭐ 4.5 · 1,642 reviews 🅿️ Free parking
💡 Visitor Tips
  • Dress code strictly observed — no shorts, sleeveless tops, or revealing clothing inside the temple
  • No pets allowed inside
  • Free incense provided for prayers — a beautiful cultural experience
  • The sea view from the temple grounds is superb — perfect for photos
  • Visit before 3 PM to enjoy the full afternoon light on the temple facade
  • Phone: +63 72 242 2533 for inquiries

🌅 Sunset Picnic at Food Cartel San Fernando
📍 Sea & Sky Hotel, Quezon Ave, San Fernando City, La Union

The perfect final chapter of your La Union trip. The Food Cartel sits right along the seaside on Quezon Avenue in San Fernando — with tables literally by the waves. Best time to visit is during sunset, when the sea turns gold and the breeze is perfect. The food is excellent (seafood especially), service is warm, and the vibe is pure La Union magic. ⭐ 4.3/5 from 81 reviews.

💰 Dinner Budget: ~₱250–500/person 🕓 Open until 10:00 PM ⭐ 4.3 · 81 reviews 🅿️ Parking available
🍽️ What to Order
  • Fresh seafood — fish, shrimp, and shells are standouts
  • Pasta dishes and lutong-bahay options for the group
  • Fruit shakes and local refreshers (try the fresh buko/coconut shake)
  • Request outdoor seating — the beachside tables are worth waiting for
  • Note: WiFi not available; Globe signal may be spotty
