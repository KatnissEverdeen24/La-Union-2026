⛪ Manaoag Church — Pangasinan
📍 Map

The Minor Basilica of Our Lady of the Rosary of Manaoag is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in the Philippines. Arriving at 4 AM is ideal — the church opens at 4 AM, the atmosphere is peaceful and deeply spiritual before the crowds arrive. Rest, pray, and enjoy your baon breakfast in the grounds or nearby.

🆓 Free entrance ⏰ Opens 4:00 AM daily 🕯 Candles available for purchase 🚫 Pets not allowed inside
💡 Tips
  • Parking is NOT inside the church — look for pay parking lots nearby (₱50–₱100)
  • Dress modestly (no sleeveless, shorts); cover-ups may be required
  • Restrooms are available on the grounds — great spot to freshen up before continuing
  • Small souvenir shops and eateries open early near the church

🏄 Seabuds — Urbiztondo Beach, San Juan
📍 Map

Seabuds is the heart of the Urbiztondo surf scene — a beloved beach café inside San Juan Surf Resort known for its healthy smoothie bowls, ube lattes, and laid-back ocean vibes. Set up your camping chairs under their signature orange umbrellas, enjoy a light breakfast, and hit the waves. Urbiztondo Beach is the best surfing spot in La Union and beginner-friendly.

🥗 Must-try: Ube Latte, Funky Monkey, Vegan Tapa 🏄 Surfing lesson: ~₱500–₱800/hr incl. board 🪑 Camping chairs: Free with Seabuds order ⏰ Opens 8 AM 📶 Has WiFi & sockets
💡 Tips
  • Arrive by 7:30 AM for the best spot under the orange umbrellas before it gets crowded
  • Surfing lessons run ~1 hour; book with beach instructors directly on the sand
  • Rash guards recommended — May sun in LU is intense
  • Swim in designated zones; strong currents possible outside surf areas
  • Explore the strip of cafés and surf shops along Urbiztondo Road before leaving
  • Seabuds food takes ~15 min — order as soon as you sit

🏝 Immuki Island — Balaoan, La Union
📍 Map

Immuki Island (also spelled Imooki) is a hidden gem off Balaoan, La Union — featuring stunning lagoons, rock formations, sea urchins, and calm waters. Arrive for lunch, swim in the lagoon, and join the island tour in the afternoon. The locals are famously warm and helpful. No liquor allowed, but BBQ grilling is permitted on the island.

🚤 Boat: ~₱200–₱300/person round trip 🏝 Island entrance: ~₱50–₱100/person 🍖 BBQ grilling allowed; bring your own food ⏰ Open 6 AM – 6 PM 🪨 Rocky terrain — wear reef shoes!
💡 Tips
  • Wear reef shoes or rubber slippers — no sandy beach, lots of sharp rocks and coral
  • Visit the lagoon earlier (before 1 PM) when it's low tide — you can walk in; afternoon = high tide
  • Bring your own packed lunch or snacks; limited vendors on the island
  • Life vests available; non-swimmers can still enjoy the lagoon safely
  • Don't touch or step on coral reefs — practice eco-tourism
  • No liquor allowed on the island

☕ The Mansion La Union — San Juan
📍 Map

The Mansion is a mountaintop retreat in San Juan with a breathtaking overlooking view of Urbiztondo Beach and the sea. A coffee shop is open where you can enjoy iced lattes and light drinks while watching the sunset paint the horizon. One of the most Instagram-worthy spots in La Union.

☕ Coffee: ~₱120–₱200 | Drinks: ₱150+ ⚠️ Very steep road going up — drive carefully! 🌅 Best sunset views in La Union 🪜 Pedestrian stairway also available
💡 Tips
  • The road up is extremely steep in 3 sections — ensure your vehicle is in good condition, engine and brakes especially
  • Arrive before 5:30 PM to secure a good spot for sunset
  • Limited seating — weekends get busy; arrive early
  • Hotel and restaurant sections may open — check latest status
  • Bring a light jacket — it's breezy up top!

🌅 MOCH Cafe & Bistro — Tuba, Benguet
📍 Map

MOCH Cafe and Bistro is one of Baguio's most spectacular dining spots — perched in Suello Village, Tuba with a jaw-dropping panoramic view of the lowlands, Irisan Valley, and even the distant La Union coast on clear days. The perfect sunset dinner to close your trip. Arrive before 5 PM to secure coveted outdoor seats.

🍕 Must-try: Pizza, Mushroom Soup, Prawns, Matcha 💰 Budget: ~₱400–₱700/person ⏰ Opens 10 AM Fri–Sun (10 AM–10 PM) ⚠️ Arrive BEFORE 5 PM for outdoor seats ⏳ Food takes 30–40 min on peak days
💡 Tips
  • Come before 5 PM — outdoor seats fill up fast on weekends, especially for sunset
  • Parking is limited; carpool or drop off at the main road and walk in
  • Order as soon as you're seated — food can take 30–40 mins on busy Saturdays
  • Bring a jacket — it gets cold at this elevation, especially after sunset
  • On clear days, you can see La Union and the ocean from your table — full circle moment! 🌊⛰
  • Phone: +63 966 824 8985
