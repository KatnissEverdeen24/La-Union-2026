The Long Road North

Friday, May 15, 2026 · Quezon City → La Union

🚗 Depart from 106 Metom St., Commonwealth, Quezon City — midnight departure (with NLEX/SCTEX tolls). Estimated drive: ~4 hours to Pangasinan.

Stop 01 · Pangasinan
Minor Basilica of Our Lady of the Rosary of Manaoag
Milo St., Manaoag, Pangasinan
Open in Maps

A revered pilgrimage site and minor basilica, home to the miraculous image of Our Lady of the Rosary. The church opens as early as 4:00 AM, making it perfect as a pre-dawn spiritual stop on your northward journey. The atmosphere before sunrise is especially solemn and deeply moving — candle vendors line the path and the soft light adds to the sacred feel.

🕓 Open 4:00 AM – 9:00 PM daily ⭐ 4.8 (10,000+ reviews) ₱ No entrance fee 💡 No pets inside
Local Tips
  • Arrive right at 4 AM to avoid the crowds that build up by mid-morning
  • No on-site parking — find paid parking on surrounding streets
  • Buy candles from vendors outside and light them at the candle area
  • Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered) as a sign of respect
~5:30 AM
🚗 Drive north from Manaoag to Luna, La Union (~1.5 hrs). You'll cross into Ilocos Region — enjoy the scenic coastal highway as the sun rises!
7:00 AM
Stop 02 · Luna, La Union
Baluarte Watchtower
Gen. Peralta St., Luna, La Union
Open in Maps

A remarkable 400-year-old circular watchtower built from coral blocks, adobe, and bricks bound with lime and egg whites — one of La Union's most fascinating historical landmarks. Originally used by pre-Hispanic inhabitants and later by the Spanish to protect coastal towns from pirates, it also served as a WWII communication post for American forces. The tower stands about 6–7 meters high near the pebble beach, offering great photo opportunities and a glimpse into the region's layered history.

🕓 Open 24 hours ⭐ 4.5 (2,100+ reviews) ₱ ₱20 Environmental Fee (covers other LU tourist spots)
Local Tips
  • Arriving early (7 AM) means fewer people and better photos — it gets crowded on weekends
  • Keep your ₱20 environmental fee receipt — it may be accepted at other Luna tourist spots
  • Local guides will offer to take photos; you can politely decline if you'd prefer to explore freely
  • There's a steak restaurant and food area nearby — but you'll be brunching soon at Kabsat!
8:00 AM
Stop 03 · Luna, La Union
Bato de Luna Art Gallery & Pebble Beach
Luna, La Union (near Baluarte Watchtower)
Open in Maps

Two gems in one area: Bato de Luna is an outdoor art gallery featuring striking concrete sculptures and murals set within a rustic, well-maintained garden — a creative fusion of art and nature that makes for great photos. Just steps away is the iconic Nalvo Pebble Beach — unique even among Philippine beaches for its multicolored smooth stones. The rhythmic sound of waves crashing against the pebbles is genuinely hypnotic. The water is crystal-clear, though waves can be strong.

🕓 Art Gallery: 5:30 AM – 6:00 PM ⭐ 4.3 Gallery · 4.8 Pebble Beach ₱ ₱50 entry + ₱20 environmental fee (Gallery) ₱ Pebble Beach: free from road / ₱50 via resort
Local Tips
  • Nearby "Bahay na Bato" (House of Stones) is reportedly more impressive — consider visiting if time allows
  • Collect a few pebbles as souvenirs — they're beautifully multicolored!
  • Not recommended for young children to swim here due to strong waves
  • You can park along the road and walk to the beach without paying the resort fee
10:00 AM
Stop 04 · Balaoan, La Union
Almeda Man-Made Forest
Bacnotan–Luna–Balaoan Rd, Balaoan, La Union
Open in Maps

A scenic stretch of tall mahogany trees arching over the highway — this is a drive-through attraction that creates a magical tunnel of green canopy. It's a popular photo stop en route south toward San Juan. The shade is refreshing and the dense rows of trees create a beautifully atmospheric corridor. Quick 15–20 minute stop — just pull over, snap your shots, and enjoy the cool forest air.

🕓 Open 24 hours (roadside) ⭐ 4.6 (273 reviews) ₱ Free ⚠ Active road — stay on the shoulder, don't stand in the middle
Local Tips
  • Best as a quick 15–20 min photo stop — don't linger long on an active highway
  • Early morning light filtering through the trees creates a dreamy, dappled effect
  • Perfect stretch-your-legs break between Luna and San Juan
10:30 AM
Stop 05 · San Juan, La Union
Kabsat La Union — Brunch
San Juan, La Union (beachfront)
Open in Maps

A highly-rated Filipino beachfront restaurant that's become a must-visit institution in La Union. Kabsat ("Friend" in Ilocano) is an open-air space with natural ventilation and big fans — it captures the relaxed coastal vibe of Elyu perfectly. The food is excellent Filipino fare; everything is consistently praised for quality and generous servings. Expect a wait on weekends — it's that popular.

🕓 8:00 AM – 12:00 AM daily ⭐ 4.1 (2,500+ reviews) ⚠ CASH ONLY — no credit cards accepted ₱ Parking: ₱150 (small lot)
Local Tips
  • Bring cash — they don't accept cards
  • Arriving at 10:30 AM is smart — before the weekend lunch rush hits
  • No aircon — open-air with fans; dress for the beach heat
  • Order the seafood dishes — fresh catches are their strength
  • Request a table facing the beach for the best view
12:00 PM
🏠 Head to Auntie Ludy's place in Camansi Area, San Fernando, La Union — unload and freshen up. Rest period 1:00–4:00 PM.
4:30 PM
Stop 06 · San Juan, La Union
The Mansion Estates
Talogtog (Upper), San Juan, La Union
Open in Maps

A hilltop destination perched above San Juan with sweeping, breathtaking views of the coastline and the La Union surf town below. The Mansion features a coffee shop and cocktail bar with an incredible panoramic vantage point — ideal for the golden hour. The views are absolutely stunning especially as the sun begins to lower, with the Pacific shimmering in the distance. The coffee and drinks are a bonus; the view is the real star.

⭐ 4.5 (66 reviews) ⚠ Very steep driveway — requires confident driver 💡 Best visited 4:30–6 PM for golden hour views
Local Tips
  • The road up has 3 extremely steep sections — ensure your vehicle is in good condition and your driver is experienced
  • A pedestrian stairway is available for those who prefer to walk up
  • Go before 5:30 PM to secure good seats before the sunset crowd arrives
  • Coffee and limited food available; hotel/restaurant amenities were expected to open by early 2026
  • Bring a light jacket — the elevation makes it noticeably cooler and breezy
6:00 PM
Stop 07 · San Juan, La Union
Tavern by the Sea · Flotsam & Jetsam · Urbiztondo Night Scene
MacArthur Highway, San Juan, La Union
Tavern Maps

KM 277 Tavern by the Sea is a large, lively open beachside venue with multiple food stalls, a bar, DJ, and live band — the vibe is vibrant and festive, especially on Friday nights. Very breezy beachside seating. After dinner, stroll along the strip to Flotsam & Jetsam — the iconic hostel-bar that defines the Elyu nightlife experience, with bonfires, live music, and a true surf-town atmosphere. Friday/Saturday nights here are legendary.

🕓 Tavern: Open 24 hrs · Flotsam: Fri–Sat 8 AM–12 AM ⭐ Tavern 4.3 · Flotsam 4.1 ⚠ Flotsam is closed Tuesdays — but Friday you're good!
Local Tips — Flotsam & Jetsam
  • Come early on Friday nights — popular spots on the sand fill up fast
  • Order at the bar and pick up your food yourself — it's part of the vibe
  • The food here is genuinely great: thai fish curry, samosas, spring rolls
  • Bring cash for both venues; card acceptance can be inconsistent
  • Bathrooms at Tavern could use improvement — use facilities before you arrive
8:30 PM
🏠 Head back to Auntie Ludy's in San Fernando (~20–30 min drive). Rest up — big surf morning tomorrow!

Stop 08 · Urbiztondo, San Juan
Surfing Lessons + Beach + El Union Coffee + Explore Urbiztondo
Urbiztondo Beach, San Juan, La Union
Beach Maps

A 4-hour block for the full Elyu experience. Urbiztondo Beach is the surf capital of La Union — consistent waves make it ideal for first-timers. Surf schools along the strip offer beginner lessons with board rental. After surfing, visit El Union Coffee — a globally-recognized café with an unbeatable beach-town vibe, famous for their Dirty Horchata and grilled cheese. Also check out Mebuyan's Vessel, the striking large-scale art installation on the beach (note: may still be under repair after Typhoon damage in late 2025).

₱ Surf lesson: ₱400–₱600/hr approx. (board + instructor) 🕓 El Union Coffee: 7:00 AM – 9:00 PM (Fri–Sat until 10 PM) ⭐ El Union 4.4 · Beach 4.9
Local Tips
  • Book surfing lessons in advance on weekends — boards and instructors fill up fast
  • El Union must-orders: Dirty Horchata (iced, creamy, espresso-spiked) + Grilled Cheese with sriracha
  • Apply waterproof sunscreen before hitting the water — May sun is intense
  • Mebuyan's Vessel is accessible via the narrow path beside Kabsat's wall fence (beach side)
  • Rinse off at beach showers before entering cafés and shops
  • The strip is walkable — park once and explore everything on foot
11:00 AM
🍽️ Head back to Auntie Ludy's for lunch. Pack up all belongings. Rest until 2:00 PM before heading to Agoo.
2:00 PM
Stop 09 · Agoo, La Union
Agoo Eco Park — Picnic & Sunset Watch
Sta. Rita, Agoo, La Union
Open in Maps

A stunning man-made agoho (she-oak) forest that opens into the coast — one of La Union's most serene and underrated spots. Towering trees line up in majestic rows leading toward the sea, creating one of the most photogenic forest-meets-beach scenes in northern Luzon. Perfect for a picnic, leisurely stroll, and settling in to watch what locals say is a genuinely beautiful sunset over the water. Much less crowded than San Juan.

🕓 Open 24 hours ⭐ 4.4 (608 reviews) ₱ ₱20 entrance fee 💡 Sunset here is reportedly spectacular
Local Tips
  • Bring a mat and picnic spread — there's plenty of shaded ground under the trees
  • Golden hour starts around 4:30–5:00 PM; arrive by 2 PM to enjoy the park fully first
  • Restroom facilities are limited — plan accordingly before you arrive
  • Bring bikes if possible — there are cycling trails through the forest
  • The beach section is right beside the forest — you can walk to the shore easily
5:00 PM
🚗 Drive to Amagbagan, Pozzurubio, Pangasinan via the fastest no-tolls inland route (~1.5 hrs). Scenic mountain/countryside drive through Pangasinan.

Stop 10 · Pangasinan
Visit Relatives in Pozzurubio
Amagbagan, Pozzurubio, Pangasinan
Search Maps

A warm family visit before the long haul home. Pozzurubio is a quiet municipality in Pangasinan known for its cool highland-influenced climate and agricultural scenery. Rest, have a merienda or dinner, and recharge before the drive back to Manila.

💡 Fill up the gas tank here before heading to SCTEX ⚠ Depart by 8 PM to avoid late-night fatigue
Homebound Tips
  • Take SCTEX/TPLEX/NLEX back to Manila (with tolls) — fastest route home
  • Estimated drive back: 4–5 hours depending on traffic
  • Stock up on Pangasinan pasalubong — bagoong, longganisa, or tupig rice cakes
  • Designate a well-rested driver for the night drive home
  • Load up on coffee or energy drinks at a gas station before departure
8:00 PM
🌙 Hit the road back to Manila via SCTEX/TPLEX/NLEX (with tolls). Estimated arrival in Quezon City: ~12:00–1:00 AM. Safe travels! 🤙
